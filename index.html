Program 01 · 60–90 minutes

AI Basics for Everyday Life

Artificial intelligence is already part of your daily life — from your phone's autocomplete to the recommendations on Netflix. But what actually is it? And how do you use it on your own terms?

This workshop demystifies AI in plain English. We'll explore popular tools like ChatGPT and Google Gemini through live demos, practical exercises, and plenty of time for questions. You'll leave knowing exactly how to use AI to make your day-to-day tasks easier — safely and confidently.

  • What AI is — and what it definitely is not
  • Live walkthrough of ChatGPT and Google Gemini
  • How to write prompts that actually get useful results
  • Using AI for writing, planning, and everyday questions
  • How to fact-check AI responses and spot hallucinations
  • Privacy: what these tools do (and don't do) with your data

Program 02 · 60–90 minutes

Cyber Safety 101

Online scams cost Canadians over $530 million in 2022 alone — and seniors are disproportionately targeted. But staying safe online doesn't have to be complicated.

This workshop walks through the most common threats people face online — from phishing emails to fake tech support calls — and gives you clear, practical steps to protect yourself. We'll go through real examples, hands-on exercises, and a checklist you can start using today.

  • How to spot a phishing email or scam text message
  • Creating strong passwords — and actually remembering them
  • Setting up two-factor authentication on your accounts
  • Understanding privacy settings on your phone and apps
  • What to do if you think you've been scammed
  • Safe online shopping and banking practices

Program 03 · 60–75 minutes

Online Kindness Challenge

The internet connects billions of people — but that connection comes with real responsibilities. Cyberbullying, harassment, and digital unkindness are more common than most people realize, and their effects are just as real as anything that happens offline.

This empathy-driven workshop is designed for youth and school groups. Through discussion, real scenarios, and interactive activities, participants explore what it means to be a good digital citizen — and what they can do when they witness or experience unkindness online.

  • What cyberbullying is — and how it differs from offline bullying
  • The real impact of online words on mental health
  • Bystander responsibility: what to do when you see it happen
  • How to report and block on major platforms
  • Building a positive digital footprint
  • The Online Kindness Pledge — a group commitment activity

Our story

Hi, I'm Gurnoor.

I started Tech Literacy Network because I watched technology widen the gap between the people who understood it and the people who didn't — and I didn't think that was okay.

Growing up, I noticed something that bothered me: the people most likely to be scammed online, most likely to feel confused or left behind by technology, were often the people no one was teaching. My grandparents. My neighbours. Seniors in my community who had built entire lives before smartphones existed and were now expected to navigate a world that had digitized almost overnight.

At the same time, I was learning about AI, cybersecurity, and digital literacy — and I kept thinking: this doesn't have to be complicated. These concepts, when explained in plain language with real examples and no condescension, are things anyone can understand. The barrier isn't ability. It's access.

"The internet is safer when everyone understands it. Tech Literacy Network exists to make that a reality — one workshop at a time."

Tech Literacy Network runs free, hands-on workshops for seniors, youth, and community groups in Brampton and the surrounding Peel Region. Every session is designed around the people in the room: no jargon, no assumptions, no rushing. Just honest, practical learning.

We're early. We're growing. And if you're reading this — whether you run a senior center, a school, a library, or just want to learn — I'd love to bring a workshop to your community.
